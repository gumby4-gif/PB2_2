Project North Campus Gateway

Status: Completed · Owner: Erin Amanda Schulz · Bunny Hill Native Landscapes

Overview

Located in Ann Arbor, MI, the entryway to the University of Michigan's North Campus has long been a complex planning challenge. University planners' goals are to transform the gateway into a sustainable, civically engaging, and collegiate entryway offering superior visual interest to those entering North Campus — while maintaining safe access to buried infrastructure along the median. Focusing on the large grassy median and connecting sidewalks along Bonisteel Boulevard, the design uses interweaving ribbons of colorful native vegetation as a metaphor for the coming together of North Campus's four major disciplines: Music, Art/Architecture, Engineering, and Campus Housing. Each color symbolizes one discipline, and the color-coordinated planting design extends beyond the initial entryway to provide seasonal wayfinding and a sense of cohesion across the campus.

Design Goals

Transform the North Campus gateway into a sustainable and visually engaging collegiate entryway · Use planting as a creative solution to the median's safety and maintenance constraints · Represent the four North Campus disciplines — Music, Art/Architecture, Engineering, and Campus Housing — through color-coordinated native plantings · Provide seasonal interest across spring, summer, and autumn · Maintain easy access to buried infrastructure · Create a sense of community and cohesion among the disparate sections of North Campus.

Current Status

Design development is complete. The planting plan features twelve native species along Bonisteel Boulevard including New England Aster, Blue False Indigo, Blazing Star, Joe-Pye Weed, Fall Obedient Plant, Yellow Coneflower, Indian Grass, and Prairie Redroot, among others. Seasonal diagrams illustrate spring, summer, and autumn bloom sequences. The ribbon planting concept extends the color-coded wayfinding system throughout the full North Campus corridor.
