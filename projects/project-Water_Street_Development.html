Project Water Street Redevelopment

Status: Completed · Owner: Erin Amanda Schulz · Bunny Hill Native Landscapes

Overview

The Water Street Redevelopment area is a 38-acre, City-owned site adjacent to Historic Downtown Ypsilanti, situated between Ann Arbor and Detroit Metro Airport. Bounded on the north by 1,500 feet of Michigan Avenue and on the south and west by a half mile of Huron River frontage, the site is redesigned as a vibrant mixed-use district. The plan provides a public plaza for year-round use, an industrial artist-themed character district along the historic railroad and new B2B trail extension, a recreation center and riverfront park, 180,000 square feet of commercial space, 784 housing units, and 1,044 parking spaces in addition to on-street parking.

Design Goals

Site the new Eastside Washtenaw County recreational building for downtown Ypsilanti to maximize accessibility · Create a small character district just outside of the current downtown that includes both commercial and residential units · Create a new riverfront park linking Riverside Park and Water Works · Connect the B2B walking and biking trail to the site and the rest of the city · Integrate surrounding street and planning patterns into the design.

Current Status

Design development is complete. The master plan organizes the site into distinct zones including Parks & Recreation, Mixed-Use, Live-Work Studios, Fishing Boardwalk, Residential, and a Recreation Center along the Huron River. The public plaza district features an elevated central green, life-size chess board, rain garden with native plantings, open-air market pavilion, and underground parking garage with 160 spaces. The mixed-use commercial corridor along Michigan Avenue is planned for ground-floor retail, second-floor office, and upper-floor residential, topped with green roofs and supported by stormwater catchment and filtration below the plaza.
