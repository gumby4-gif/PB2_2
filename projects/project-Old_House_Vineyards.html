Project Old House Vineyards

Status: Completed · Owner: Erin Amanda Schulz · Bunny Hill Native Landscapes

Overview

Located in the heart of Virginia's wine country within the quiet county of Culpeper, Old House Vineyards has become a well-known staple for exquisite wines, beautiful scenery, and world-class large events. After broadening their scope in 2014 to include the production of both wine and spirits on the property, up-to-date and eye-catching signage was commissioned to encompass both aspects of the business. Taking into account the newly widened main drive, sunny location, and relaxed country atmosphere, the main sign was kept to a simple faux stone wall with complementary base plantings providing year-round interest. Mirrored wall sections and planting plans provide symmetrical balance to either side of the drive, while specially designed alcoholic-process inspired signage provides wayfinding — exposed copper piping reminiscent of large liquor stills points toward the new distillery, while a flattened wooden barrel indicates the winery's tasting room.

Design Goals

Commission eye-catching entrance signage that reflects both the winery and distillery sides of the business · Complement the widened main drive with a balanced, symmetrical front gate design · Use faux stone construction and base plantings to provide year-round visual interest · Incorporate alcoholic-process inspired wayfinding elements — copper pipe for the distillery, wooden barrel for the tasting room · Maintain a relaxed, country atmosphere consistent with the vineyard's character and setting.
