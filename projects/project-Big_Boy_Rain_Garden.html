Project Big Boy Rain Garden

Status: Completed · Owner: Erin Amanda Schulz · Bunny Hill Native Landscapes

Overview

Located in northeast Ann Arbor, the Plymouth Road Big Boy restaurant is working to reduce stormwater damage, pollution, and waste traveling across the site's parking area. Current water flow is creating significant damage to the existing asphalt lot, requiring annual patches. Solutions include the installation of permeable pavers, trench drains, curb cuts, and a rain garden in key portions of the site.

Design Goals

Increase stormwater infiltration on site to decrease pollutants entering the water table · Capture the first ½″ flush · Reduce runoff damage to the parking lot · Minimize waste and disruption to existing infrastructure · Create an aesthetically pleasing design that showcases the sustainability goals of the owner · Emphasize the flow of water around the site via planting design.

Current Status

Construction documents and phasing plans are complete. Phase 1 covers permeable paving and trench drain installation along the primary parking field. Phase 2 addresses the rain garden planting and curb cut modifications at the site perimeter.
